SAIL Matrix & OSO Buckets

SAIL (Specific Assurance and Integrity Level) is determined from fGRC + rARC. Each SAIL dictates the rigor of Operational Safety Objectives (OSOs).

SAIL Determination Matrix (Annex E)

fGRC ↓ / rARC → ARC-a ARC-b ARC-c ARC-d
GRC 1 I I II II
GRC 2 I II III III
GRC 3 II III IV IV
GRC 4 II III IV V
GRC ≥5 III IV V VI

Operational Safety Objectives (OSOs)

Ground Risk OSOs
  • OSO #01: Precise and updated UAS geo-data
  • OSO #02: Reliable UAS design (ground impact)
  • OSO #03: UAS maintenance
  • OSO #04: UAS maintained in safe condition
  • OSO #05: Remote crew competency
  • OSO #06: Procedures (ConOps) designed & validated
  • OSO #07: Normal procedures (checklists, SOPs)
  • OSO #08: Emergency response plan
  • OSO #09: External services supporting safe operations
Air Risk OSOs
  • OSO #10: Detect & Avoid (DAA) capability
  • OSO #11: C2 Link performance & robustness
  • OSO #12: Loss of C2 Link contingency
  • OSO #13: Automation & failsafe design
  • OSO #14: Human-Machine Interface (HMI)
  • OSO #15: Multi-crew coordination (if applicable)
  • OSO #16: Adverse weather resilience
Adjacent Area & Operational OSOs
  • OSO #17: Traffic information & airspace awareness
  • OSO #18: Crew coordination with ATC
  • OSO #19: Safe recovery from technical failures
  • OSO #20: Operational limitations & restrictions compliance
  • OSO #21: Adverse operating conditions mitigation
  • OSO #22: Operational control & monitoring
  • OSO #23: Adjacent area/third-party risk management
  • OSO #24: Security (cyber, physical, data protection)

OSO Rigor by SAIL

  • SAIL I–II: Low rigor (self-declaration, documented procedures)
  • SAIL III–IV: Medium rigor (design assurance, validation, independent review)
  • SAIL V–VI: High rigor (certification-level assurance, EASA approval)
