ARC Engine (Annex C - Airspace Risk Class)

Airspace Risk Class (ARC) is determined by airspace characteristics and operational context. Based on JARUS SORA Annex C.

Airspace Encounter Categories (AEC 1-12)

AEC Description Baseline iARC
AEC 1 Uncontrolled, Visual Meteorological Conditions (VMC), Low density ARC-a
AEC 2 Uncontrolled, VMC, Medium density ARC-b
AEC 3 Uncontrolled, VMC, High density ARC-c
AEC 4 Controlled, VMC, Low density ARC-b
AEC 5 Controlled, VMC, Medium density ARC-c
AEC 6 Controlled, VMC, High density ARC-d
AEC 7-12 Instrument Meteorological Conditions (IMC) scenarios ARC-c to ARC-d

ARC Step #5: Tactical Mitigation Credit

If VLOS AND Low Time Exposure (controlled airspace, low traffic density):
Residual ARC = Initial ARC − 1 class

Example: AEC_9 → iARC = ARC-c → with Step #5 → rARC = ARC-b

Airspace Containment

Containment Effect on ARC
None No credit
Horizontal May reduce ARC (Annex C logic)
Vertical May reduce ARC (Annex C logic)
Horizontal & Vertical Maximum credit (full containment)
